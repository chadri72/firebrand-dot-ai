TRANSPORTATION & LOGISTICS

Generative AI to Improve Parcel Delivery Customer Satisfaction

We built a state-of-the-art NLP model to associate international customs tax codes with layman's terms and product descriptions.

The Challenge

One of the largest US-based global logistics providers in the world is using AI, Natural Language Processing (NLP), Semantic Analysis and Topic Modeling amongst other approaches to completely redefine the process and experience with which consumers and businesses ship packages everyday.

This was a mammoth undertaking considering they are solely responsible for delivering an average of 14 million packages across 220 countries and territories every day. Historically, one of the more complicated aspects of international shipping is customs clearance, but this company is using data and analytics to turn this into a market differentiator and a game-changer in the International Express segment.

The Solution

The solution required building a big data environment for analytical modeling that scales up to enterprise needs, adheres to industry standards and involves agile delivery teams. Today, the company has their first models into production with the first iteration of the analytics architecture, and a dozen new models in development coinciding with the next iteration of the analytics architecture this year. The company expects the data science development to scale up in the coming years to handle over 50 time-series, AI and ML/neural-network models spanning all facets of international shipping.

The Results


The client saw impact across several areas:

  • Significant improvement in B2C and B2B customer experience navigating the multitude of forms required to ship a parcel internationally (e.g. CSat, Net Promoter, etc.)
  • Achieved 94 - 97% customs clearance success rate (i.e. parcels are not caged, delayed or returned to send by customs).
  • 35 - 45% reduction in current manual efforts required to address parcel caging risk by customs.
